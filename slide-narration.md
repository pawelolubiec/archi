## Slide 01 — A hybrid digital ecosystem

Milarex operates as one group, but value is created across a connected network: customers and sales offices around the world, the factory in Poland, and the systems that support every commercial and operational decision.

<break time="0.7s" />

Our digital strategy is to make that network work as one business. Not by forcing every process into one large system... but by connecting the right specialist tools through a common data model.

<break time="0.7s" />

In this ecosystem, MiFo brings demand and margin insight from the market. Production systems control what happens in the factory. The E R P provides a reliable financial core. A shared data platform creates one version of the truth, and artificial intelligence adds prediction and decision support across the group.

<break time="0.7s" />

This is a deliberately hybrid architecture: modular enough to change, disciplined enough to govern, and scalable enough to support growth.

<break time="1.4s" />

The principle is simple: one group... one trusted data model... and the best system for each job.

## Slide 02 — Why now

The case for change begins with the cost of the current state.

<break time="0.7s" />

Today, sales, production, warehouse, quality, and finance work with data held in separate systems. Reports are assembled manually. Teams spend time reconciling numbers instead of acting on them. By the time yield, machine effectiveness, or margin becomes visible... the opportunity to improve the outcome may already have passed.

<break time="0.7s" />

The problem is not only technical. Different sites and departments follow different processes for the same decisions. Custom connections make every system change slower and more fragile. Margin is often reconstructed after the event — rather than seen when a quote is made, or while production is still running.

<break time="0.7s" />

These constraints have a direct business cost: slower decisions... avoidable working capital... operational risk... and lost margin.

<break time="1.4s" />

Doing nothing is therefore not the low-risk option. The cost of inaction grows with every quarter in which the business becomes larger, more complex, and harder to coordinate.

## Slide 03 — Architecture

The target architecture starts with the business... not with software.

<break time="0.7s" />

Across the top are the eleven processes that run Milarex, from market demand and forecasting through production, quality, logistics, finance, and continuous improvement. Each process is supported by three connected layers.

<break time="0.7s" />

Specialist applications execute the work. The shared data platform turns their data into consistent definitions, trusted key performance indicators, and one group-wide view. Above that, artificial intelligence provides prediction, simulation, anomaly detection, and automation.

<break time="0.7s" />

The change from the current state to the target state is fundamental. Today, information is fragmented, and many handoffs are manual. In the target model, operational data moves through standard connections into a governed platform — while insight and decision support flow back into the processes where action is taken.

<break time="1.4s" />

This is not a proposal for one E R P to own everything. It is a flexible ecosystem aligned with how Milarex actually creates value: connected by data... governed by process... and built to evolve.

## Slide 04 — Initiatives portfolio

The strategy becomes executable through seven initiatives, organized in a deliberate order.

<break time="0.7s" />

First, we build the core. P T S strengthens production control and live performance visibility. The new E R P establishes a stable financial foundation. Master data governance gives the group consistent products, costs, customers, and definitions.

<break time="0.7s" />

Second, we improve decisions. MiFo develops forecasting, planning, and profitability insight. P I D becomes the trusted source for product, recipe, specification, and packaging data.

<break time="0.7s" />

Third, we protect adoption. A structured people-and-change program ensures that new capabilities are used consistently. Cybersecurity and business continuity protect operations, data, and future growth.

<break time="0.7s" />

This sequence matters. Technology alone does not create value. Trusted data, clear ownership, capable users, and resilient operations must advance together.

<break time="1.4s" />

The portfolio therefore connects every initiative to a business outcome: stronger profitability, better margin control, and faster portfolio development. Build the core first... improve decisions next... and protect adoption throughout.

## Slide 05 — A decision layer on top

Notice the band at the top of the architecture. Just like our digital strategy target landscape, there are three layers: operational applications at the bottom… one shared data layer… and on top, a decision layer — the Control Tower — where alerts, A I recommendations, simulations and human approvals live.

<break time="0.7s" />

Every signal lands here. Demand variances. Capacity squeeze. Ordering deadlines. Quality and farm flags. Thin shelf-life buffers. Alerts from every system meet in one place — each with a deep link into the application where the answer lives. This is the decision layer from the strategy architecture… made real.

<break time="0.7s" />

The generative A I agent turns those signals into a concrete, quantified proposal — with the profit impact computed by the same engine as the budget. It can even warn about side effects. And one thing it cannot do… is publish anything on its own.

<break time="0.7s" />

A human clicks Approve. Then every plan updates itself: the forecast publishes… the budget re-projects… production, purchasing and staffing stay consistent. No e-mails. No re-typing. Every gate is a human decision — with a name, a timestamp, and a system.

<break time="0.7s" />

And before you commit, the margin simulator runs the same calculation chain as the budget — so management sees the impact first… not in next quarter's results.

<break time="1.4s" />

Automation does the work. Accountability stays with people. That is the target architecture: applications… one data layer… and a decision layer on top.

## Slide 06 — The factory’s internal systems

The factory is where the architecture becomes operational value — and where the Control Tower gets its signals.

<break time="0.7s" />

The chain begins with MiFo, bringing demand, customer orders, and margin intelligence from the sales organization. P T S tracks production as it happens — every kilogram, every order, every line, and every shift — making yield, O E E, and traceability visible in real time.

<break time="0.7s" />

P I D provides one controlled source for recipes, specifications, allergens, and product versions. W M S connects warehouse locations and stock rotation directly to production. Q M S and LIMS capture quality checks and laboratory results at the source. The E R P closes the financial loop through the ledger, valuation, and group reporting.

<break time="0.7s" />

Each system has a clear responsibility. Together they connect demand, material, production, quality, inventory, and money — and they feed the shared data layer that powers the Control Tower above.

<break time="1.4s" />

The objective is not one system for the whole factory. It is one coherent operating model — specialized systems below, and one decision layer on top.

## Slide 07 — Building order in the organisation

The same factory can operate in two very different ways.

<break time="0.7s" />

In the current state, the systems exist, but information often stops between them. Spreadsheets, email, and manual re-entry bridge the gaps. Exceptions surface late — in meetings, not in a Control Tower. Material continues to move… while the information needed to manage it arrives late, or in conflicting versions.

<break time="0.7s" />

In the target state, one connected chain runs from order to output. Demand from MiFo informs production. P I D supplies the correct recipe and specification. P T S records execution and traceability. Quality, warehouse and finance stay in the loop. Signals rise into the Control Tower — and approved decisions flow back down, without re-typing.

<break time="0.7s" />

This is what building order in the organisation means: clear system responsibilities… standard data connections… and a governed decision layer above the hall.

<break time="1.4s" />

The result is faster coordination, stronger control, and fewer manual handoffs — linked specialist systems below… Control Tower on top. Not one operational monolith.

## Slide 08 — Business impact

<break time="0.7s" />

This model links every target to the systems and processes that can deliver it. Better yield comes from live production data in P T S, accurate product data in P I D, commercial insight in MiFo, and a shared data platform. Higher forecast accuracy comes from MiFo, advanced planning, trusted data, and artificial intelligence. Lower inventory comes from warehouse control, production planning, and a reliable financial core.

<break time="0.7s" />

The current working targets show the scale of the opportunity: yield, from eighty-two to eighty-seven percent... O E E, from seventy-one to eighty-two percent... forecast accuracy, from sixty-four to eighty-two percent... customer service, from ninety-four to ninety-eight percent... and inventory, from thirty-eight to twenty-nine days.

<break time="0.7s" />

These figures must be validated with Controlling and the data platform before they become commitments. But the accountability model is already clear.

<break time="1.4s" />

Every key performance indicator has an owner... a baseline... a target... and named delivery levers. That is how technology investment becomes measurable business performance.

## Slide 09 — Roadmap 2026 to 2030

The roadmap is designed as a sequence of controlled investments... not a big-bang transformation.

<break time="0.7s" />

We begin with data foundations, integrations, and a tightly defined E R P scope. In parallel, we strengthen the operational core through MiFo, W M S, P T S, and P I D. Once trusted data and core processes are in place, we expand planning, quality, analytics, automation, and ultimately digital-twin capabilities.

<break time="0.7s" />

The tracks can overlap because several core systems are already live or in development. A data-platform-first approach reduces rework in later phases. A focused financial E R P limits scope. Parallel delivery, supported by clear stage gates, allows Milarex to move faster — without losing control.

<break time="0.7s" />

The standard path runs through twenty thirty, with the E R P planned to be live by the end of twenty twenty-eight. An accelerated path can bring much of the twenty-thirty scope forward... provided the organization has the capacity to execute.

<break time="1.4s" />

The message is discipline with momentum: deliver value in stages... protect dependencies... and make every phase earn the next.

## Slide 10 — What we need from the board

The strategy is ready to move from direction to execution. Three board decisions are required.

<break time="0.7s" />

First, approve the digital strategy: the target operating model, the hybrid architecture, and the principles that will guide decisions through twenty thirty.

<break time="0.7s" />

Second, approve the execution roadmap: the priorities, sequence, milestones, and dependencies that convert the strategy into a controlled delivery program.

<break time="0.7s" />

Third, approve the investment and governance model: the funding envelope, accountable portfolio owners, steering structure, key performance indicators, and board-level reporting needed to keep delivery focused.

<break time="0.7s" />

These approvals do not authorize an uncontrolled technology program. They establish the mandate and guardrails for detailed planning, stage-gated investment, and transparent measurement. Each initiative remains accountable for a defined business outcome, and each subsequent funding decision can be based on evidence.

<break time="1.4s" />

The choice before the board is therefore clear: align on the direction... authorize the roadmap... and establish the governance required to execute with confidence.
